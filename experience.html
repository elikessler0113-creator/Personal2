Musical Ensembles & Experience

Throughout my life, I have consistently been enrolled and involved with some form of music education. Whether it be piano lessons, concert band, marching or pep band, music theory, or percussion ensemble, my commitment to music performance and education remains unwavering.

Piano

From the age of five until I was 15, I took piano lessons weekly during the school year. Throughout those years, I participated in numerous recitals and state competitions, even advancing to the final round many times and being one of 24 winners one year.

Concert Band

Starting in fifth grade and continuing through my first semester of college, I played in my school's concert band. In high school, I auditioned for and was accepted into the upper-level band. We frequently performed at contests and consistently received the highest possible scores.

Drumline

In grades 10, 11, and 12, I auditioned for and played in the Chaska High School Drumline. We would meet once or twice every week during the summer to rehearse our cadences and prepare for the marching band season. It was our job to keep the band in time and keep everyone on track during parades.

Marching Band

In addition to Drumline, I was also a part of the Chaska High School Marching Band. We performed at football games, marched in parades, and presented a themed halftime show. Marching band also required daily rehearsals before classes started in the weeks leading up to Homecoming.

Pep Band

When the marching band season concluded, I performed in the Chaska High School Pep Band. We played at numerous sporting events, cheering on the teams and performing high-energy music to entertain the fans.

Percussion Ensemble

Beginning in my first semester of college, I joined the University of Minnesota Duluth Percussion Ensemble. We rehearse twice a week and perform one concert every semester. We collaborate in both small and large groups to present a diverse range of percussion music for each performance.
